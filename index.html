Quick Start

The package provides two RMarkdown documents with full analysis of a single or a group of repertoires. User just need to change paths in this documents to their MiTCR data and have the knitr package installed. Analysis of a single repertoire stored at "/inst/library.report.Rmd" and included . Analysis of a a group of repertoires stored at "/inst/crossanalysis.report.Rmd" and included .

Examples

Load the list with data frames with beta chain data and V- and J-genes alphabets for human subjects:

data(twb)
data(human.alphabets)

Get numbers of clones, in-frames and out-of-frames, unique amino acid CDR3 sequences and other basic statistics:

mitcr.stats(twb)

Compute V-usage of the first subject:

freq.Vb(twb[[1]])

Compute V-usage of all subjects and plot a histogram:

vis.V.usage(twb, .dodge = T)

Compute a pairwise number of shared clones and plot a heatmap:

twb.int <- intersect(twb, 'n0e')
vis.heatmap(twb.int)
